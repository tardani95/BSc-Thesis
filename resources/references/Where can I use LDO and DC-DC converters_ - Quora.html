Where can I use LDO and DC-DC converters?

Low-dropout regulator or LDO is linear voltage regulator. It consist of a amplifier & FET (Field Effect Transistor).

DC-DC converters (Buck for stepping down) are class of SMPS, usually consist of a diode, a inductor & a FET.

LDO is cheaper than DC-DC converter. Also LDOs do not generate switching noise unlike the DC-DC converters. LDO can regulate voltage even when the output voltage is very close to input voltage. But while regulating, LDO transfer the excess power into heat, which is heat loss. So you can use LDO if you want to change a nominal amount of voltage. But if you want to change a large number of voltage (say 12V to 5V), the efficiency is very very low (41% for 12V to 5V) & also you may need heat sink. So it is not recommended to use LDO for a high difference in input & output voltage unless efficiency & heat is not a big concern. It is applicable for all the linear voltage regulators. However for a DC-DC converter, the efficiency is very much higher, because it lowers the voltage by increasing the current (like transformers). So the output power is nearly similar to that of input power even if the output voltage is much lower than input power. It achieves at least 80% efficiency (sometimes 95%) in most cases. But it is little costlier than linear voltage regulators. Mobile Power banks uses DC-DC converters because efficiency & heat is a big concern (even they can get 75% efficiency by using linear regulators).

So in a nutshell, if you want to change the voltage little bit, use LDO & if you want to change the voltage in a large number, use DC-DC converter (Buck Converter for step down & Boost Converter for step up).
