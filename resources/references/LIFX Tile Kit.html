LIFX Tile Kit has 3M Command™ Hooks on the back. These will stick to flat surfaces such plaster walls, sealed timber and glass. Leaving the two halves of the Command™ Hook on your LIFX Tile Kit, remove the release liner and with gentle pressure push the LIFX Tile Kit onto the wall in the desired spot.


LIFX Tile Kit is not suitable for outdoor use or exposure to wet conditions. This also means it is not suitable for use in bathrooms and other areas with high moisture levels.


You should not install your LIFX Tile Kit on a ceiling or in an upside down position. While the 3M adhesive binds strongly to surfaces it is recommended that you install your LIFX Tile Kit in a safe area.

Cons: too expensive for what you get.
Only 5 tiles.
Poor design on tile power/daisy chain placement as you only have to ports to connect to each other, so to can only go let to right, instead of have a simple four ports to stack them in a much more versatile way.
No good software or third party apps like the bulbs. And if finally, I have to say it again. They are just too darn expensive for how little you get. Clearly they rushed these out, while screwing us early tech adopters. I can NOT recommend these at all right now. Hold off and get the new Nano leafs that were displayed at CES this year. They're pretty. That's about all the good I can say

Patrick on Feb 26, 2018

After a total of 6 hours trying every conceivable method of connecting them (guided by the support guide, and suggestions from a tech guy), I managed to get all five to group twice. When I tried to find a consistent way of keeping them connected so I could actually put them on my wall they always collapsed back to the Single Red Dot.

Never once was it as simple as "reset and turn off the main tile, connect it to your network, turn it off and plugin the 4 new tiles." At best it was unchain all the tiles, connect the base tile, turn it off, plug a single new tile in, turn it on, and make sure it's connected, turn off, repeat 3 more times, and if you get really lucky you end up with all five connected. Just don't disconnect them (even if you pull the power first).

My fingertips feel like they've been shredded thanks to all the sharp edges on every surface of the tiles. My excitement has turned into a simmering hatred of the demons that live within the tiles. I've completely lost 3/4ths of a work day worth of time.

My recommendation is stay far far away from these if you value your body, your emotions, or your time.
